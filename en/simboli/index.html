Explanation of Symbols

The parchment
It symbolizes the figure of Cicero, representing knowledge, eloquence, and the transmission of culture. It recalls the value of the written word and thought, central elements in the work of the Roman orator.

The bunch of grapes
It recalls the figure of the Maenad, a symbol of vitality, intoxication, and instinct. It represents the link to the cult of Dionysus, evoking primordial energy, freedom of expression, and surrender to the passions.

The Cardinal's hat
It symbolizes the figure of the Cardinal of York, recalling ecclesiastical authority, prestige, and his prominent role within the Church hierarchy. It also represents the spiritual and political dimension that characterized his historical figure.
