1. Fragment of Nike Tauroctona

I come from a weary time. For years, Rome was torn apart by civil wars: citizen against citizen, violence against violence. The people were exhausted, longing for peace. In that climate, to put an end to the chaos, many accepted what would have previously seemed impossible: the rule of a single man. Thus, in the name of stability, the balance of the Republic was cast aside. A dilemma for all time: to give up a portion of freedom in exchange for security, or to defend the principles of the Republic, the balance of powers, citizen participation, and the rejection of tyranny? This slab fragment comes from the theater of Tusculum. Look at the relief: a Nike tauroctona, a winged Victory in the act of sacrificing a bull. This is not a mere decoration, but a highly symbolic image. These images spoke to the crowd more than many speeches: the bull represents primordial force, wild energy, and sometimes the chaos of war, while Nike, Victory, embodies the order imposed by the winning power. The act of sacrifice therefore alludes to the submission of untamed forces and the birth of a new order, desired and imposed by Octavian Augustus. An order that promised peace and stability, but which also arose from the end of republican freedom and the establishment of a central power. As for me… I could never accept it! I defended the Republic as long as I had a voice to speak and breath to live. And if this cost me my life, so it had to be: because nothing is sweeter than liberty!
